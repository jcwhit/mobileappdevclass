Some other Sun is an easy to pick up role playing game system that focuses on a character’s weapon instead of the character themselves. Character creation is a point system with players starting out with a 100 points to spend on their weapon. The point values range with the most effective options are the most expensive.

Steps

0) Resolve surprise attacks.
1) Take initiatives.
2) Highest initiative goes first.
3) Resolve actions:
  • Movement or Reaction
  • Attack or Ability
  • Easy actions
4) Go to next initiative and repeat 3 until last initiative and jump back to 2.

Look below for a more descriptive explanation of each step.

Combat Zones

Combat zones represent areas of combat. They dictate the range of weapons and melee combat.
  • To attack in melee, the target needs to be in the same area as the attacking character.
  • To make a ranged attack, the target needs to be within effective range of the weapon and have line of sight of the target.
A zone should include information such as:
  • Cover
  • Items
  • Border to show the area
Zones should be divided into effective sizes and have large areas divided into smaller zones.
ZONE PICTURES TO COME

Surprise attacks occur when the target is totally unaware of your presence. The attacker doubles their rep bonus to the hit role. This can also occur after initiatives have been determined. (Example).

Initiative is called when combat has begun and the GM has called for it. It is a d20 + Nerves score + Rep + other bonuses.
This represents how ready to act a player or enemy is. It determines play order.

Movement and Reaction

Each round(after all initiatives have gone) a character can either choose to move the number of zones on their character sheet or use their reaction.
Reactions: attack a character leaving the zone you are in, perform a reaction only action, hide(d20+sense+rep), take cover, or use an item.

Attack or Ability

Attack:
Melee: d20 + beef + rep* VS Target Toughness
Ranged: d20 + sense + rep* VS Target Toughness
Ability:
Perform an ability from an archetype or character description.
Other:
Persuading or intimidating a character.
Reloading a weapon.

Easy Actions

Easy actions are actions that don't require much effort such as; talking to a party member, handing an item to a party member in the same zone as you, etc.

Weapons

Weapons in Some Other Sun make the character. All stats are generated when the weapon is created.
Players will have 100 point to spend on weapon creation.
More powerful bonuses require more points.

Example:

Jimmy is a revolver carrying son of a gun. His weapon is able to fire several different types of calibers and rounds. Jimmy’s has taken on the role of an Ace, which is role that specializes in focusing on large amounts of damage to a single target.

The Gun:

-10 shot magazine: 10 pts
-Dynamic chamber: any standard small arms round can be shot from it 10pts
-QuickLink sights: able to take accurate shots quickly +1 to sense 20pts
-Calligraphic name inscription: ornate decoration that is appealing +1 to Grit 20pts
-Advanced rifling: +1 to sense 20 pts
-Smokey Black Paint: +1 to grit 20pts

Final Stats:

Grit: 2
Smarts: 0
Beef: 0
Sense: 2
Nerves: 0

Players are only limited to their imagination. Guns can shoot lighting shurikens if they want. Or a rocket launcher that shoots missiles that explode into acid.

Reputation

Street cred, this is added to each roll and represents character advancement. All players start a reputation 1.
  • reputation is computed as the difference of good rep and bad rep
  • rep = the higher of the two - the lower of the two
  • players start out by picking which one will start at 1
  • good rep is gained by doing good deeds
  • bad rep is gained by doing evil deeds
  • increases are determined by the GM and should be increase only by significant acts

Stats:


*Grit: this is your stat for social

*Smarts: books, how knowledgeable about the universe
-history, science, languages

*Beef: how stronk

*Sense: how well you can hit or notice something

*Nerves: how quickly you react; initiative, dodging, etc.

Combat:

-Melee: Beef + rep

-Ranged: Sense + rep

-Toughness: Armor + Nerves or Beef

-Mobility: nerves/2 = # of zones can move in a turn
